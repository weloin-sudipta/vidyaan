# Exam Workflow

## Overview
Comprehensive workflow for examination management from creation to result publication, involving teachers, students, and administrators.

## Workflow Stages

### 1. Exam Planning & Creation
**Actors:** Teachers, Administrators
**Purpose:** Define examination parameters and schedule

#### Process Steps:
1. **Exam Creation**
   - Teacher selects exam type (Unit Test, Mid Term, Final Exam)
   - Defines syllabus coverage and learning objectives
   - Sets passing criteria and grading scale

2. **Schedule Definition**
   - Set exam date, start time, and duration
   - Define exam venue/room allocation
   - Consider student availability and conflicts

3. **Resource Preparation**
   - Prepare question papers and answer keys
   - Arrange required materials (calculators, charts)
   - Coordinate invigilator assignments

#### Validation Rules:
- Exam dates must not conflict with holidays
- Duration must be reasonable for content coverage
- Room capacity must match student count

### 2. Student Notification & Preparation
**Actors:** System, Students
**Purpose:** Inform students and allow preparation time

#### Process Steps:
1. **Automated Notifications**
   - System sends exam schedule via SMS/email
   - Publish on student portal and notice board
   - Include exam guidelines and requirements

2. **Student Preparation**
   - Access to syllabus and sample papers
   - Clarify doubts with subject teachers
   - Submit any special requirements (extra time, etc.)

#### Timeline Requirements:
- Minimum 7 days notice for major exams
- Minimum 2 days notice for unit tests
- Emergency exam notifications within 24 hours

### 3. Exam Execution
**Actors:** Students, Teachers, Invigilators
**Purpose:** Conduct examination with integrity

#### Process Steps:
1. **Pre-Exam Setup**
   - Student verification and seating arrangement
   - Distribution of question papers and materials
   - Final instructions and rules reminder

2. **During Exam**
   - Time management and progress monitoring
   - Address student queries appropriately
   - Maintain exam integrity and security

3. **Post-Exam Collection**
   - Systematic collection of answer sheets
   - Verification of submission completeness
   - Secure storage of exam materials

#### Quality Controls:
- Random invigilator supervision
- CCTV monitoring where available
- Student identity verification
- No communication devices allowed

### 4. Evaluation & Grading
**Actors:** Teachers, Administrators
**Purpose:** Fair and accurate assessment of student performance

#### Process Steps:
1. **Answer Sheet Distribution**
   - Assign papers to qualified evaluators
   - Ensure blind evaluation where possible
   - Provide clear marking schemes

2. **Grading Process**
   - Systematic marking with rubrics
   - Double-checking of high-stakes papers
   - Calculation of marks and percentages

3. **Result Compilation**
   - Aggregate marks across subjects
   - Calculate grade points and rankings
   - Prepare result reports and analytics

#### Quality Assurance:
- Inter-rater reliability checks
- Spot checking by senior teachers
- Appeal process for disputed grades
- Audit trail of all changes

### 5. Result Publication & Analysis
**Actors:** Administrators, Students, Parents
**Purpose:** Communicate results and identify improvement areas

#### Process Steps:
1. **Result Verification**
   - Administrative review and approval
   - Final quality checks before publication

2. **Publication Process**
   - Release results on student portal
   - Send individual result notifications
   - Publish class-wise and school-wide statistics

3. **Performance Analysis**
   - Subject-wise and topic-wise analysis
   - Identification of teaching gaps
   - Student performance trend analysis

#### Communication Channels:
- Digital portal for detailed results
- SMS alerts for result availability
- Physical report cards distribution
- Parent-teacher meetings for discussion

## Role Responsibilities

### Teachers
- Create and schedule exams
- Prepare question papers and keys
- Conduct fair evaluation
- Provide feedback and guidance

### Administrators
- Oversee exam scheduling
- Ensure resource availability
- Monitor evaluation quality
- Handle appeals and disputes

### Students
- Prepare adequately for exams
- Follow exam rules and ethics
- Review results and seek clarification
- Use feedback for improvement

### Parents
- Support student preparation
- Review results and provide guidance
- Participate in parent-teacher discussions

## Exception Handling

### Exam Postponement
- Weather/natural disaster scenarios
- Student health emergencies
- Technical/logistical issues
- Minimum 24-hour notice requirement

### Malpractice Cases
- Immediate suspension from exam
- Investigation by committee
- Disciplinary action based on severity
- Alternative assessment arrangements

### Result Disputes
- Formal appeal process within 7 days
- Re-evaluation by different teacher
- Final decision by exam committee
- Transparent communication of outcomes

## Quality Metrics

### Process Metrics
- On-time exam completion rate
- Student satisfaction scores
- Evaluation accuracy rates
- Appeal resolution time

### Outcome Metrics
- Pass/fail rates by subject
- Grade distribution analysis
- Improvement trends over time
- Learning objective achievement
